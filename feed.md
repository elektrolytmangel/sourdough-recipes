# Feeding Your Sourdough Starter

Feed your starter at least **every 2 weeks** to keep it alive and active.

---

## Formula — 1:5:5 (starter : water : flour)

| Ingredient                                | Amount  |
| ----------------------------------------- | ------- |
| Starter                                   | 5g      |
| Water (lukewarm, 35–38°C)                 | 25g     |
| Flour (50% whole wheat + 50% white wheat) | 25g     |
| **Total output**                          | **55g** |

All 55g goes back into the fridge for storage.

---

## Steps

1. Transfer 5g of your starter into a clean jar.
2. Add the water and stir until the starter is fully dissolved.
3. Add the flour and mix well until no dry flour remains.
4. Cover loosely and let rise at room temperature (22–26°C) for **8–12 hours**, until roughly doubled in volume.
5. Seal and refrigerate. The starter can rest for up to **2 weeks** before the next feed.

> **Tip:** Classic sourdough starter is more active than a stiff Italian-style starter. In a warm kitchen (>25°C) it may double in as little as 2–3 hours — watch the volume, not the clock.
