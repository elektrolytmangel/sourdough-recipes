# Bake Formula — Building a Levain

Use this whenever you want to bake. It follows the same 1:5:5 ratio as the regular feed, but scaled up so you have enough for baking **and** enough left over to keep your starter going.

---

## Formula — 1:5:5 (starter : water : flour)

| Ingredient                                | Amount   |
| ----------------------------------------- | -------- |
| Starter                                   | 10g      |
| Water (lukewarm, 26–30°C)                 | 50g      |
| Flour (50% whole wheat + 50% white wheat) | 50g      |
| **Total output**                          | **110g** |

| Split                            | Amount |
| -------------------------------- | ------ |
| Use for baking (→ see `bake.md`) | 75g    |
| Return to fridge as starter      | 35g    |

---

## Steps

1. Transfer 10g of your starter into a clean jar.
2. Add the water and stir until the starter is fully dissolved.
3. Add the flour and mix well until no dry flour remains.
4. Cover loosely and let rise at room temperature (22–26°C) for **8–12 hours**, until doubled and at its peak (domed top, bubbly, slightly tangy smell).
5. Use **75g** in your bread recipe.
6. Seal the remaining **35g** and refrigerate — this is your starter for the next bake.

> **Tip:** Use the levain at its peak for the best rise. If it has collapsed it is past its peak — you can still bake with it but the rise will be weaker.
