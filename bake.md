# Baking a Sourdough Loaf

Yields **~1 loaf, ~800g**.  
Total flour: ~500g | Hydration: ~70%

---

## Ingredients at a Glance

### Levain

Prepare this first — see [bake-formula.md](bake-formula.md).

|                  |     |
| ---------------- | --- |
| Levain (at peak) | 75g |

### Autolyse

| Ingredient      | Amount      |
| --------------- | ----------- |
| Flour           | 500g        |
| Water (26–30°C) | 300g – 350g |
| Salt            | 10g         |

> Start with 300g water (80%) and add the rest gradually — absorption varies by flour type.

**Flour mix used:** 10% whole wheat · 30–40% white wheat · 50–60% Ruchmehl

| Flour       | Swiss name   | German type  | Character                                                             |
| ----------- | ------------ | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Whole wheat | Vollkornmehl | Vollkornmehl | 100% of the grain; dense and nutty                                    |
| White wheat | Weissmehl    | Type 405     | Light, strong, neutral — standard bread flour                         |
| Ruchmehl    | Ruchmehl     | Type 1050    | Partially whole grain; earthy, flavourful; typical Swiss baking flour |

> The 75g levain already contains ~37.5g flour and ~37.5g water, bringing totals to **>500g flour** and **~350g water** (~70% hydration).

---

## Process

### 1. Prepare the Levain

Follow [bake-formula.md](bake-formula.md). Allow **8–12 hours** at 22–26°C until doubled and at its peak. Use 75g; refrigerate the rest.

### 2. Autolyse — optional (60 min)

> **You can skip this step.** Autolyse helps the flour absorb water faster and makes kneading easier, but it is not required.

If you choose to do it: mix the 462g flour and 312g water together — just combine until no dry flour remains, do **not** knead. Cover and rest for **60 minutes**.

### 3. Build the Main Dough (~10 min)

1. Combine the flour (or autolyse dough), water, and 75g levain. Knead for **5 minutes**.
2. Add the salt and knead for another **5 minutes** until the dough is smooth and elastic.

### 4. Bulk Fermentation (2 hours)

Cover and let the dough rest at room temperature (22–26°C) for **2 hours**.  
Perform **2-3 rounds of stretch & fold**, roughly every 30-50 minutes:

- Grab one side of the dough, stretch it up, fold it over to the opposite side.
- Rotate the bowl 90° and repeat — 4 folds = 1 round.

### 5. Shape & Proof

Shape the dough into a round (boule) or oval (batard) and place it seam-side up in a floured proofing basket (banneton).

| Method                                 | Time                    |
| -------------------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Room temperature (22–26°C)             | 3–6 hours (not tested)  |
| Refrigerator (5-7°C) — **recommended** | 10–18 hours (overnight) |

Cold proofing gives a better ear, more complex flavour, and makes scoring easier.

### 6. Bake

1. **Preheat** your oven to **250°C** (top/bottom heat) with **two baking sheets** inside:
   - One on the **top rack** — for steaming.
   - One on the **second-lowest rack** — for the bread.
2. Turn the dough out onto the hot lower baking sheet. **Score** the top with a sharp blade or bread lame (one confident slash at ~45°).
3. **Steam:** carefully pour hot water onto the top baking sheet to fill the oven with steam. Use hot water so it steams immediately.
4. Bake at **250°C for 20 minutes**, keeping the steam in.
5. Open the oven door, let the steam escape, and take out the top baking sheet. **Reduce the temperature to 220°C** and continue baking.
6. Bake for another **25–30 minutes** until the crust is deep brown and the loaf sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.
7. Cool on a wire rack for **~1 hour** before cutting.
